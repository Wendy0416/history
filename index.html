Palace

Foundation of the Palace Museum-Forbidden City The Forbidden City was the imperial palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties (1368-1911 AD) in China.
According to the ancient Chinese astrological theory, Ziweiyuan (the North Star) was located in the middle of the sky. After taking the throne, Zhu Di, the third emperor of the Ming Dynasty, decided to move his capital to Beijing. The palace began to be built in 1406, and it was completed in the 18th year of Yongle in the Ming Dynasty (1420).
In 1911, the 1911 Revolution overthrew China's last feudal monarchy, the Qing Dynasty. In 1924, Qing Fei Emperor (Xuantong Emperor) Aixinjueluo Puyi was expelled from the palace.

故宫为中国明、清两代（公元1368～1911年）的皇宫,故宫又称紫禁城. 1911年，辛亥革命推翻了中国最后的封建帝制——清王朝，1924年清废帝（宣统帝）爱新觉罗·溥仪被逐出故宫.

Museum

After the victory of the 1911 Revolution in 1911, the Qing government announced its abdication. The palace was supposed to be nationalized. However, according to the "Preferential Treatment Conditions of the Qing Dynasty" drafted by the Provisional Revolutionary Government at that time, Emperor Puyi was allowed to "temporarily stay in the palace." That is, the "backbed (inner court)" at the back of the Forbidden City. At that time, the government decided to move the cultural relics of the Rehe (Chengde) Palace (ie Chengde Mountain Resort) and the Shengjing (Shenyang) Forbidden City to the "Qian Dynasty (Outer Dynasty)" section of the front half of the Forbidden City, and the Antiquities Exhibition was established in 1914.

1911年辛亥革命胜利后，清王朝政府宣布退位，这座宫殿本应全部收归国有，但根据当时临时革命政府拟定的《清室优待条件》，逊帝溥仪却被允许“暂居宫禁”，即紫禁城后部的“后寝（内廷）”。当时的政府决定，将热河（承德）行宫（即承德避暑山庄）和盛京（沈阳）故宫的文物移至故宫前半部的“前朝（外朝）”部分，于1914年成立了古物陈列所.

History

On the eve of the full-scale outbreak of the Anti-Japanese War, the Japanese imperialists swallowed the territories of Northeast China. After the September 18th Incident in 1931, they stepped into the Beijing area. In order to protect the safety of cultural relics, the Palace Museum transported cultural relics to Nanjing.
After the founding of the People's Republic of China, the staff of the Palace Museum in Beijing worked with a brand-new mental outlook. They clear weeds, dredge rivers, and clean up garbage. On October 2nd, 2017, the Palace Museum realized the first online ticket sales and convenient for tourists to visit.

抗日战争全面爆发前夕，日本帝国主义者吞并了中国东北地区。在1931年9月18日事件之后，他们进入了北京地区。为了保护文物安全，故宫博物院将文物运至南京。 中华人民共和国成立后，北京故宫博物院的工作人员有了崭新的精神面貌。他们清除杂草，疏通河流，清理垃圾。 2017年10月2日，故宫博物院实现了首次在线售票,方便了对外展览.
